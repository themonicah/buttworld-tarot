# VI - The Lovers

## Characters: Romeo & Juliet

### Quotes
- **Upright:** "Wherefore art thou, rear-meo?"
- **Reversed:** "A plague on both your cheeks"

### Tarot Meaning
The Lovers represents relationships, choices, alignment of values, and love. Reversed: disharmony, imbalance, misalignment.

### Composition

**Scene:** The iconic balcony scene. Juliet on a Renaissance balcony draped in vines, Romeo below in a moonlit garden. Verona architecture in background.

**Central Figures:** Two butt figures reaching toward each other - Juliet-butt leaning over balcony rail, Romeo-butt reaching up from below. The space between them is the tension.

**Juliet-Butt Design:**
- Elegant, soft, youthful butt
- Long flowing Renaissance dress/gown
- Hair suggestion flowing down (maybe a veil)
- One hand reaching down
- Dreamy, yearning posture

**Romeo-Butt Design:**
- Athletic, passionate young butt
- Renaissance doublet and tights
- Sword at hip
- One hand reaching up
- Dramatic, devoted stance

**Background:**
- Moonlit Verona balcony
- Climbing roses and ivy
- Stars above
- Garden below
- Maybe an angel figure above (classic Lovers card)

**Symbolic Elements:**
- Angel above blessing/cursing them (fate)
- Tree of knowledge and tree of life (one on each side)
- Sun and moon (day/night, Montague/Capulet)
- Rose with thorns (love and pain)

### Color Palette

### Mood
